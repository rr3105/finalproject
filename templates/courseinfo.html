My favorite course at Columbia:

Of all the classes I've taken in the last four years at Columbia, it's hard for me to come up with one absolute favorite. Instead, I have a few. First, I really liked University Writing as a freshman. I feel that the class honed my writing skills and made me aware of political/social issues in a way I hadn't been before. Next, I really enjoyed taking Intro to Java with Adam Cannon. The class exposed me to object-oriented programming and thinking big picture before delving into smaller details to solve each problem, and I also enjoyed the CS theory-related parts of that class. Finally, Econometrics with Jushan Bai was a great class for me. Though difficult, I learned a lot about how to make connections between phenomena in the real world and quantifying the above with data and models. All in all, Columbia has provided me with a solid foundational education, and I look forward to the next phase in my intellectual life and career.
